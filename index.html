Hammer EQ User Manual

Welcome to the official companion manual for the Hammer sound tuning suite. Hammer EQ enables audiophiles and sound producers to write directly to their device's DSP audio registers to achieve high-fidelity acoustic performance.

1. The Five-Band EQ Framework

The Hammer hardware console divides the audible spectrum into five distinct core frequency channels, each representing a crucial dynamic range:

60Hz • Sub-Bass

Controls the deep weight and physical rumble of kick drums, bass synths, and heavy sub-acoustic waves. Boosting this creates a visceral bass feeling without cluttering mid frequencies.

230Hz • Low-Mid Frequency

Defines the warmth, fullness, and body of guitars, cello, low piano strings, and male vocals. Too much of this band creates muddy, echoing soundscapes.

910Hz • Midrange Clarity

The vocal frequency zone. Houses the fundamental frequencies of spoken words, lyricists, and lead acoustic lines. Boosting this elevates vocal legibility over backtracks.

4kHz • Upper-Mid & Presence

Controls attack transient sounds like cymbal hits, guitar plucks, and high harmonics. Essential for bringing instruments closer or pushing them further back into the soundstage.

2. Active Noise Control Modes

Hammer active wireless devices support dedicated physical hardware acoustic filtering, toggleable via the ANC Mode selectors:

  • Normal Mode: Disables both passive transparency and active feedback, allowing the earbuds to rely purely on passive silicone seal and run at standard low-power footprints.
  • Ambient Transparency Mode: Passes external frequency waves safely through the dual array microphone into the sound stream, enabling environmental awareness while running.
  • Active Noise Cancellation (ANC): Leverages dual array feed-forward and feed-back digital microphones to generate anti-noise waves, isolating the listener from background environment rumble.

3. Dynamic Hardware Handshake

Unlike standard software visualizers, Hammer EQ syncs with the device's internal state. Saving or tweaking sliders forces a real-time serial Bluetooth data command, adjusting the hardware parameters of the coaxial drivers directly.

For any technical support, ensure your device is running the latest firmware v1.0.4.
